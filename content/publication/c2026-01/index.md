abstract: |-
  As offshore wind power advances to far-reaching sea areas, traditional manual operation and maintenance is plagued by harsh conditions, high risks, low efficiency and severe data isolation. Aiming at the technical bottlenecks of poor environmental adaptability, low autonomous inspection precision and inadequate remote operation capability of robots in the high-humidity, high-salt-spray and strong wind-wave marine environment, this paper studies the key technologies of an intelligent inspection and teleoperation maintenance robot system for offshore wind power platforms. We design a high-reliability system including three kinds of robots, with composite adaptive anti-disturbance components for specific inspection purposes. A multi-modal intelligent inspection system is constructed with spatiotemporal adaptive denoising perception, cross-modal transfer detection and cloud-edge collaborative deployment to improve defect identification performance. A cloud-edge-end integrated platform is built and verified in wind turbine nacelles and booster stations. Experimental results show the system meets key indicators: repeated positioning accuracy ≤±5mm, equipment state recognition accuracy ≥98%, master-slave attitude deviation ≤±1°, response delay ≤200ms, IP65 protection grade and manipulator end load ≥3kg. It can increase inspection frequency by over 5 times and cut labor costs by 60%~80%, significantly improving the intelligent operation and maintenance level of offshore wind power assets. This research fills the domestic technical gap in far-reaching offshore wind power intelligent inspection and maintenance robots, providing technical support for safe and efficient operation of offshore wind power platforms, with broad prospects in offshore oil and gas platforms and nuclear power plant auxiliary plants.
