abstract: |-
  Coal conveying system operation and maintenance (O&M) has long faced idler monitoring challenges: traditional fixed sensors fail to stably capture idler-specific vibration signals and cover idler arrays inadequately on dynamic equipment, leading to missed early idler faults. To address this, this paper develops a dedicated distributed optical fiber vibration sensing system for idler fault inspection. It collects subtle idler vibration characteristics (e.g., abnormal frequencies from bearing wear or jamming) via optical fibers and uses deep learning to extract idler fault features — enabling real-time, accurate localization of incipient idler faults, overcoming manual inspection limits, and enhancing O&M performance, reliability, and cost-efficiency. To mitigate open-pit coal yard noise (from machinery vibration, wind, material impact) masking weak idler signals, the system integrates the improved AI algorithm Bi-ACGAN. This filters background noise while retaining critical fault signals to boost feature recognition efficiency and accuracy. Laboratory tests (simulating coal yard conditions with controlled idler wear/jamming) and on-site verification at a large thermal power plant show the system notably improves O&M safety, efficiency, and intelligence: it shortens idler fault detection time by over 60% vs. manual patrols, cuts idler-induced unplanned maintenance costs by ~35%, and has strong scalability to support idler monitoring digital transformation in energy, mining, and port bulk material scenarios.
