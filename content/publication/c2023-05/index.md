abstract: |-
  Conventional concentration sensors have severe limitations in terms of detection accuracy and information acquisition. To solve this problem, this paper proposes a novel microwave-based detection system with a sensor structure of 16-electrode ring sensor that can alternate between transmitting and receiving states via a switch matrix. The system can effectively detect the concentration of pulverized coal under complex coal airflow conditions, greatly enhancing the accuracy of concentration parameters. Experimental results demonstrate that the system can accurately classify concentrations of 0.2, 0.5, and 0.8 (kg/kg) of pulverized coal using a concentration classification algorithm based on an support vector machine(SVM) model. Furthermore, the study highlights the significance of using more electrodes as it significantly improves the accuracy of measurement results.
