abstract: |-
  As a subfield of Semi-supervised Learning (SSL), Semi-supervised Object Detection (SSOD) has become a prominent research area in deep learning. Most existing studies focus on a two-stage detection framework, while the one-stage oriented such as YOLO series-based methods remain underexplored. This gap arises from the distinctive anchor design paradigms of YOLO and its end-to-end architectures, which complicate the application of SSOD strategies developed for two-stage models. In this paper, we propose a novel one-stage SSOD approach which called SSOD-YOLO. It is built upon the student–teacher mutual learning paradigm. In order to boost the model performance, the Dynamic Scheduling Strategy (DSS) and Augmentation-Guided Pseudo-label Optimization (AGPO) were proposed to refine the pseudo-label quality. The results have been experimentally verified on the open-source PASCAL VOC dataset using different proportions of labeled and unlabeled data, confirming that the YOLOv8 outperforms the One Teacher and Unbiased Teacher by 1.7% mAP50−95 and 3.9% mAP50−95 respectively, demonstrating that our SSOD-YOLO achieves more generalized and balanced results across both one-stage anchor-based and anchor-free models.
